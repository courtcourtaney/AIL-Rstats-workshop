Online resources for statistics and using R/RStudio:

Stats theory and calculators/tables for probability measurements and how tests work in-depth:
https://www.statisticshowto.com/

R specific tutorials, especially nice ggplot2 graphing tutorials:
http://r-statistics.co/

Lessons in other statistical tests with examples using the rstatix library, written by its creator
https://www.datanovia.com/en/lessons/

More on why ANOVA is actually kinda just a linear regression
https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/175246/why-is-anova-equivalent-to-linear-regression

Reporting stats in APA style journals (or PhD theses?):
http://my.ilstu.edu/~jhkahn/apastats.html

More on outliers (and good definitions for stats basics):
https://www.theanalysisfactor.com/outliers-to-drop-or-not-to-drop/
